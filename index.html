Government Website Case Study

Commission of Fine Arts

Government Landing Page Redesign
Government websites house large amounts of complex information that can be difficult to navigate. With this project I analyzed, tested and redesigned the Commission of Fine Arts landing page. I redesigned it in a way that users can intuitively navigate to the information that they need reducing frustration.

MY ROLE

This was a solo project where I completed the Reseach, Definiton, Ideation, prototyping and testing within a 4 week timeframe

TOOLS USED

FIGMA, Google Suite

THE CHALLENGE

The Commission of Fine Arts is an independent federal agency that reviews and advises on matters of design and aesthetics in the Washington DC area. Currently users are not able to easily understand the purpose of the Commission of Fine Arts nor find necessary information on the Commission of Fine Arts website based on the landing page.

GOALS FOR REDESIGN

Users will be able to easily understand the purpose of the Commission of Fine Arts
User will be able to easily understand how they can get information regarding the submission process
Users can easily find where to register for meetings

HUERISTICS ANAYLYIS

I analyzed 5 pages and the major pain points were: The homepage does not give a good overview of what the CFA is or its purpose

  • The homepage does not give a good overview of what the CFA is or its purpose
  • Color pallet is not modern or pleasing
  • Too many links to other pages, user could easily get lost
  • Too much wasted white space throughout all of the pages
  • A lot of content on each page that is not easily scannable, a lot of scrolling needed
SITE MAP

I did a card sorting exercise and used the analysis to create a site map. As a result I made the following decisions

  • I broke up the Project Review section into 3 sections for clarity
  • I separated Design Topics for About CFA section to highlight the past projects since this was so prominent on the homepage. This was one of the sections that was difficult for users to find during usability testing
  • I combined the Records and Meetings sections since records are a result of the meetings, the pages seemed to group well together

FIVE SECOND TESTING
Positives
  • Users felt the site was trustworthy
  • Users like the overall layout
Opportunities
  • Users still weren't sure who the audience is for this site
  • Users did not get a great understanding of the CFA
Solutions
  • Made the text less wordy for scanning
  • Made the text for the jurisdictions larger

CONCLUSION
  • This was a fun project - I enjoyed categorizing and organizing information to make easier to understand
  • The card sorting and site mapping exercise allowed me to take a close look at the information and helped shaped the overall organization and design
  • I think simplifying the design and taking away unnecessary clutter helps users focus more on the content and understand the purpose and workflow available on the site
